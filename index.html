Halal Food in Japan!

Visiting Japan can be a challenge for Muslims. This list of restaurants and shops selling halal foods is meant to make your visit to Japan easier and more enjoyable. There are many great restaurants with fully halal menus and many more Muslim friendly restaurants serving special halal meals. You can enjoy the tastes from home or even local flavors. Besides restaurants and shops, you can find information on local mosques and other services to make your stay enjoyable and memorable.

About the Community

There has been a strong Muslim community in Kansai for decades. The largest mosque in Japan can be found in Kobe built in 1935. The Kobe Muslim Mosque holds regular services for local muslims and visitors. The Osaka Masjid also holds services in northern Osaka. Both locations have halal markets and restaurants close by. The Osaka Masjid can even arrange delivery of halal foods to your accommodation

Useful Phrases

Be careful about pork. The Japanese do not always understand that HAM （ハム）and BACON （ベーコン）are also pork. Interestingly, the Japanese do not always associate pork （豚）with meat（肉）. You should make sure when eating in a restaurant not on our list. Japan is a secular society and many people do not understand the importance of religion. Many times it is better to explain that you are allergic to meat.

  • Niku-nashi: 肉無し・No Meat
  • Buta-nashi: 豚無し・No Pork
  • Arugi des: アールギです・It's an allergy.
